Competitions

nwHacks 2020

Organized by: nwPlus

In this hackathon my team and I built Food for Thought. Our goal was to incentivize grocery stores and restaurants to reduce food waste by donating to nearby food banks. As a byproduct, customers can also support grocery stores that are more sustainable with their left overs by viewing sustainability ratings posted on our website.

UBC Local Hack Day 2019

Organized by: nwPlus

In this hackathon, my team and I build Inky! Inky is an iOS app that records audio files, transcribes them as text files, and condenses them into a summary. It has an option to record live audio or import pre-existing audio files when choosing files to summarize. All programming is done using Swift on Xcode.

UBC Learning Data Analytics Hackathon 2019

Organized by: Learning Analytics Project, UBC LAVA, UBC CAPICO, and Sauder Learning Services

In this hackathon my team and I constructed a timetable generator which created a timetable with maximum professor grading and minimal walking distance. We implemented Google map API to retrieve distance data, web scraped data from ratemyprofessor.com to retrieve professor grading, and used Canvas’ course csv file to retrieve professor name and class location

UBC Assistive Technology Design Competition (2nd Place)

Organized by: UBC APSC 100

During my freshmen year at UBC, we were presented with this competition, challegning us to create an affordable yet effective assistive device for our Client who had lower body paralysis. Our team achieved 2nd place! and had the honourary invite to dine with the Dean of Faculty of Applied Science. Our product enhanced wheelchair mobility and enabled client to pick up keys from ground while seated on wheelchair.
