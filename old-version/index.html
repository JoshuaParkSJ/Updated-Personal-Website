The E-Z Roll

My group and I entered the APSC 100 Assistive design competition, an open competition for all APSC 100 courses, to create an assistive device for a specific client named Ken Fraser. Ken Fraser had paralysis down the his lower armpits and restricted movement in his wrists. Thus, we created a device that could enable him to not only pick up keys from the ground, but also to steer and accelerate his wheelchair forward using a rod grip with no wrist movement required. We sketched out our potential designs and used Solid Works to 3D model and print out our chosen device.

The device was successful, as we placed overall 2nd in the competition. On top of that, the 1st and 2nd places recieved an honorary invite to have lunch with the Dean of Engineering at UBC!!

Entangle

Entangle is a non-profit organization that aims to teach coding and design to newcomer youth and refugees in the Lower Mainland of Canada. The motive for this organization stems from my friend Enrique, who immigrated to Canada from El Salvador. His family didn't speak good English, thus Enrique even at a young age was pressured to take many legal responsibilities. Through perseverance and many help from his peers, he was able to thrive and make it to UBC engineering. Now, we wish to help students and immmigrants that were in the same situation as Enrique by pairing them with mentors in STEM and guiding them to the path of success.
